# FIRE Plan — Financial Independence Framework
### Matthieu & Shelby | Built 2025

---

## The goal

Reach financial independence by Matt's age 52-54 (target window: 2041-2043). Not necessarily full stop retirement — but the point where work is optional and the portfolio can sustain our lifestyle indefinitely.

This is not a fantasy number. It is a math problem.

---

## The math

**Target annual spending in retirement:** $120,000/year (today's dollars, adjusted for inflation)

**Portfolio target using the 4% rule:** $120,000 ÷ 0.04 = **$3,000,000**

The 4% rule is the widely-accepted safe withdrawal rate — the percentage of a portfolio you can withdraw annually with a high probability the money lasts 30+ years. At $3M, 4% = $120,000/year.

## The three-bucket withdrawal framework

At retirement, assets will be structured into three buckets:

| Bucket | What it holds | Job |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — Cash** | 2-3 years of expenses in HYSA or T-bills | Cover near-term spending without touching investments |
| **2 — Income** | Bonds, dividend-generating assets | Refill Bucket 1 annually |
| **3 — Growth** | QQQM, VTI, broad index funds | Long-run inflation protection and portfolio growth |

Withdrawals always come from Bucket 1. Bucket 3 is only touched in good market years to rebalance.

This structure means market downturns in early retirement do not force us to sell growth assets at a loss.

## Roth conversion strategy

Between retirement and age 73 (when RMDs begin), the window exists to convert any pre-tax 401(k) balances to Roth at a lower tax rate. Each year, model the optimal conversion amount to stay within the current tax bracket without triggering a higher rate.

This is the single highest-leverage tax move available in early retirement and is worth planning 5-10 years in advance.

## The honest constraint

The $3M target is achievable but requires consistent savings discipline for 15-18 years. The biggest risks are:

1. Lifestyle creep outpacing income growth
2. A large unplanned expense (home repair, medical, family) derailing contributions for 1-2 years
3. Underestimating healthcare costs in the gap between retirement and Medicare at 65

Each of these is manageable with the three-bucket structure and a maintained cash reserve.
