# Retirement Planning Guide

An interactive retirement planning guide built for a client (anonymized) who was experiencing retirement anxiety at 62. The goal was to give her a self-service tool she could use privately to model her own situation — without sharing exact numbers with family members or a financial advisor she had not yet met.

---

## What this is

Most retirement anxiety comes from not knowing what the numbers actually mean. This guide explains the five risks retirees face in their first five years, a three-bucket withdrawal strategy, Social Security timing, and RMDs — in plain language, with adjustable sliders so the reader can model their own situation.

It was built as a client-facing deliverable, not a personal finance tool. The design priority was trust and clarity over feature depth.

## Why this exists in a CS/IM portfolio

Customer success professionals are often the first person a customer turns to when they are overwhelmed. The job is not to be a subject matter expert in everything — it is to take something complex, understand it well enough to explain it clearly, and build the right artifact for where the customer is emotionally and logistically.

That is what this guide does.

It also demonstrates:

- **Empathy-driven design** — the slider format exists because the client was not ready to share exact numbers. The tool works around that constraint rather than fighting it.
- **Structured communication** — five distinct risks, each with a plain-language name, a one-paragraph explanation, and a mitigation action
- **AI-augmented workflow** — built with Claude as a research and writing partner; every section was iterated based on real feedback from the recipient
